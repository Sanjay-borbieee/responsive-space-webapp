EXPLORE JUPITER

Jupiter, the largest planet in our solar system, is a gas giant known for its iconic Great Red Spot and powerful storms. Explore its stunning cloud bands, numerous moons, and mysterious atmosphere.

JUPITER FACTS

  • Largest planet in the solar system
  • Diameter: 139,820 km
  • Day length: 9.9 Earth hours
  • Year length: 12 Earth years
  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen and helium, with traces of other gases
  • Surface features: No solid surface; composed mostly of gas with storms like the Great Red Spot.

JUPITER MISSION

Galileo

The first spacecraft to orbit Jupiter, studying the planet and its moons from 1995 to 2003.

Galileo made numerous discoveries about Jupiter's atmosphere, magnetic field, and its large moons, such as Europa and Io.

Juno

Launched in 2011, Juno is providing detailed information on Jupiter's atmosphere and magnetic field.

Juno's mission has revealed new insights about Jupiter's deep atmosphere, internal structure, and auroras.

Voyager 1 and 2

These twin spacecraft conducted flybys of Jupiter in 1979, providing the first detailed images of the planet and its moons.

Pioneer 10

The first spacecraft to fly past Jupiter in 1973, opening up further exploration of the outer planets.

Pioneer 10 provided the first close-up images of Jupiter and helped pave the way for future missions to the outer planets.
