EARTH FACTS

  • Third planet from the Sun
  • Diameter : 12,742 km (1.88 x Mars)
  • Day length: 24 hours
  • Year length: 365.25 Earth days
  • Atmosphere: 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, trace amounts of other gase
  • Abundant water, covering about 71% of the planet's surface.

EARTH MISSION

Apollo 11

The first manned mission to land on the Moon in 1969, returning samples and data from Earth's satellite.

Apollo 11 was the spaceflight that first landed humans on the Moon. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first people to walk on the lunar surface.

International Space Station

A habitable artificial satellite orbiting Earth, enabling research and international collaboration since 1998.

The International Space Station (ISS) serves as a laboratory for scientific research and a platform for testing technologies for future exploration missions.

Hubble Space Telescope

Providing unprecedented views of space from Earth's orbit since 1990, revolutionizing our understanding of the universe.

The Hubble Space Telescope is a space-based observatory that has transformed our understanding of the cosmos by capturing stunning images of distant galaxies, nebulae, and more.

James Webb Space Telescope

Launched in 2021, the most powerful telescope ever sent to space, providing deeper insight into the origins of the universe.

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) is a large space-based observatory designed to answer fundamental questions about the universe, studying the first galaxies and distant planets.
