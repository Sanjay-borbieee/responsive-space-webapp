SATURN FACTS

  • Second largest planet in the solar system
  • Diameter: 116,460 km
  • Day length: 10.7 Earth hours
  • Year length: 29.5 Earth years
  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen and helium
  • Surface features: Prominent ring system and many moons, including Titan.

SATURN MISSION

Cassini

A mission that studied Saturn and its rings from 2004 to 2017, providing invaluable data.

Cassini revealed stunning details about Saturn's ring structure and discovered new moons.

Pioneer 11

The first spacecraft to fly past Saturn in 1979, sending back the first images of the rings.

Pioneer 11 provided groundbreaking data about Saturn's atmosphere and its rings.

Voyager 1 and 2

These spacecraft performed flybys of Saturn in 1980 and 1981, revealing details of the planet and its moons.

Voyager's observations contributed to our understanding of Saturn's complex atmosphere and ring system.

Hubble Space Telescope

Provides continuous observations of Saturn, contributing to ongoing research.

The Hubble Space Telescope has captured remarkable images of Saturn and its rings over the years.
