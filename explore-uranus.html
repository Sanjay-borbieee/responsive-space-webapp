EXPLORE URANUS

Uranus, the seventh planet from the Sun, is known for its unique blue color and extreme axial tilt. Explore its icy composition, fascinating ring system, and intriguing moons that hold secrets about the formation of our solar system.

URANUS FACTS

  • Seventh planet from the Sun
  • Diameter: 50,724 km
  • Day length: 17.9 Earth hours
  • Year length: 84 Earth years
  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen, helium, and methane
  • Surface features: Icy composition, no solid surface, tilted on its side

URANUS MISSION

Voyager 2

The only spacecraft to visit Uranus, flying by in 1986.

Voyager 2 provided detailed images and data on Uranus’s atmosphere, moons, and rings during its flyby.

Hubble Space Telescope

Hubble has captured detailed images of Uranus and its rings over the years.

Hubble's observations have given astronomers a deeper understanding of Uranus’s weather patterns and seasonal changes.

James Webb Space Telescope

Launched in 2021, JWST continues to provide new insights into Uranus’s atmosphere and composition.

JWST is helping to explore the chemical composition and thermal structure of Uranus’s atmosphere.

Future Uranus Orbiter and Probe

Planned mission to study Uranus up close with an orbiter and atmospheric probe.

This proposed mission would provide the first in-depth study of Uranus’s atmosphere, rings, and moons.
