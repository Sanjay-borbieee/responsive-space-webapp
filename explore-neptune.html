EXPLORE NEPTUNE

Neptune, the eighth planet from the Sun, is known for its striking blue color and strong winds. Explore its icy composition, fascinating ring system, and intriguing moons that hold secrets about the formation of our solar system.

NEPTUNE FACTS

  • Eighth planet from the Sun
  • Diameter: 49,244 km
  • Day length: 16.1 Earth hours
  • Year length: 165 Earth years
  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen, helium, and methane
  • Surface features: Icy composition, strong winds, and large storms

NEPTUNE MISSION

Voyager 2

The only spacecraft to visit Neptune, flying by in 1989.

Voyager 2 provided detailed images and data on Neptune’s atmosphere, moons, and rings during its flyby.

Hubble Space Telescope

Hubble has captured detailed images of Neptune and its rings over the years.

Hubble's observations have given astronomers a deeper understanding of Neptune’s weather patterns and seasonal changes.

James Webb Space Telescope

Launched in 2021, JWST continues to provide new insights into Neptune’s atmosphere and composition.

JWST is helping to explore the chemical composition and thermal structure of Neptune’s atmosphere.

Future Neptune Orbiter and Probe

Planned mission to study Neptune up close with an orbiter and atmospheric probe.

This proposed mission would provide the first in-depth study of Neptune’s atmosphere, rings, and moons.
