EXPLORE MERCURY

Mercury, the closest planet to the Sun, is a fascinating world characterized by extreme temperatures and a cratered surface. Discover its unique features, such as the Caloris Basin and its fascinating geological history, as we unveil the mysteries of this enigmatic planet.

MERCURY FACTS

  • Closest planet to the Sun
  • Diameter: 4,880 km
  • Day length: 59 Earth days
  • Year length: 88 Earth days
  • Atmosphere: Thin atmosphere, mostly oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium
  • Surface features: Contains large impact craters and is highly cratered, with no liquid water.

MERCURY MISSION

Mariner 10

The first spacecraft to fly by Mercury in 1974, providing the first images of the planet's surface.

Mariner 10 was the first spacecraft to conduct a flyby of Mercury, capturing images and collecting data on its surface and magnetic field.

MESSENGER

The first spacecraft to orbit Mercury from 2004 to 2015, mapping its surface and studying its composition.

MESSENGER (Mercury Surface, Space Environment, Geochemistry, and Ranging) provided detailed maps and data about Mercury's geology, atmosphere, and magnetic field.

BepiColombo

A joint mission by ESA and JAXA, launched in 2018, set to arrive at Mercury in 2025 for extensive research.

BepiColombo aims to study Mercury's composition, geological history, and magnetic field, providing insights into the evolution of terrestrial planets.

Earth-based Observations

Ongoing observations and studies of Mercury from Earth-based telescopes contribute to our understanding of the planet.

Earth-based telescopes have played a crucial role in enhancing our understanding of Mercury's surface and environment through spectroscopy and imaging.
